Squamish Nights

A speckled deep green and blue glaze with a celestial look.

Named for the valley sky an hour north of the studio, where the last light pools between the mountains. The glaze breaks deep where the walls curve and scatters into fine speckle at the rim — fired in reduction, so no two pieces ever come out alike.

How it's made

Squamish Nights starts as a charcoal stoneware body, dipped twice and fired to cone 6 in reduction. Oxygen starved, the cobalt and iron in the glaze pull apart into deep blues and green-black speckle.

Where the glaze runs thick it goes nearly midnight; where it thins over an edge, the clay glows through warm. That balance can't be repeated — only invited.
