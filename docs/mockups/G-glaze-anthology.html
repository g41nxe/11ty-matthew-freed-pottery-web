Concept G — Glaze Anthology
Designer's note — palette & rationale

One idea carries this concept: the fifteen glazes are the site. They are met one at a time — a glaze walk on the homepage, a full stage per glaze behind it — never as an overwhelming wall. Organic, thrown-looking shapes frame hero and walk; piece galleries stay rectangular and calm.

Paperunglazed clay body — the page ground
Navy (sacred)anchor from his cobalt glazes — wordmark, headings
Inkhis charcoal glazes — body text
Mocha & goldhandwritten notes; sparse highlights (Strathcona/Zen gold)

Type: Yeseva One gives every glaze name the character of a collector's label, Vollkorn keeps all reading calm, Caveat appears at most once per screen — a note from the studio, not decoration. Glaze one-liners are the official collection descriptions; products and prices are real (shop.matthewfreed.net). Piece photos stand in from the existing image pool where no product shot exists.

Matthew Freed

The studio on Jackson Ave

Matthew Freed first sat at a wheel in Winnipeg in the early nineties, and has been committed to clay for good since some eighteen years. Today he makes functional stoneware in Strathcona, Vancouver — mugs, bowls, plates and vases meant to be used, not saved for good — honoured with Circle Craft's Gold Medal for Excellence in Craft.

Each piece is glazed in one of fifteen recipes developed in the studio. No two firings come out the same. That is the point.

— Matt
